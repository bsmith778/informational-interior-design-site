Popular Interior Design Styles

Not sure what your style is yet? Start here. These quick descriptions help beginners recognize what they love—and build a cohesive plan.

Modern

Clean lines, open space, and intentional decor. Modern rooms often use neutrals with a few statement pieces.

Minimalist

Calm and clutter-free. Minimalism focuses on function, airy layouts, and a few quality pieces that feel peaceful.

Boho

Warm and relaxed. Boho blends natural materials, plants, and layered patterns to create a cozy, collected vibe.

Farmhouse

Cozy and welcoming. Farmhouse mixes warm woods, soft neutrals, and rustic details for comfort that feels timeless.

Tip: Choose one main style, then borrow small details from a second style for personality without chaos.

Beginner Interior Design Topics

These are the basics that make your home look “put together.” Start with one topic and try it in a single room.

How to choose a color scheme

Choose one main neutral, a supporting neutral, and one accent.

How to measure a room

Measuring a room is essential for designing spaces. You want to measure walls, doorways, and walkways. Always compare measurements to furniture dimensions before buying to avoid cramped layouts.

How to mix patterns

Mixing patterns can elevate your style when done correctly. Start by choosing a color palette that ties patterns together, ensuring harmony. For balance, pair large patterns with smaller ones, creating visual interest without overwhelming. Keep a shared color so it feels cohesive.

How to pick the right furniture size

Leave 30–36 inches for walking paths and avoid furniture that is too small for the room.

Mistakes to avoid

Buying without measuring, rugs that are too small, and relying on one overhead light are the most common beginner mistakes.

How to create a cohesive space

Repeat 2–3 colors and 2–3 materials (wood, metal, linen). Consistency is what makes spaces feel designed.
