Design Styles

Not sure what your style is yet? Start here. These quick descriptions help beginners recognize what they love—and build a cohesive plan.

Modern

Clean lines, open space, and intentional decor. Modern rooms often use neutral colors with a few statement pieces.

Minimalist

Calm and clutter-free. Minimalism focuses on function, airy layouts, and a few quality pieces that feel peaceful.

Boho

Warm and relaxed. Boho blends natural materials, plants, and layered patterns to create a cozy, collected vibe.

Farmhouse

Cozy and welcoming. Farmhouse mixes warm woods, soft neutrals, and rustic details for comfort that feels timeless.

Tip: Choose one main style, then borrow small details from a second style for personality without chaos.

Beginner Topics

These are the basics that make your home look “put together.” Start with one topic and try it in a single room.

How to choose a color scheme

Pick 1 main neutral, 1 supporting neutral, and 1 accent. Repeat the accent 3+ times so the room feels intentional.

How to measure a room

Measure walls, doorways, and key walkways. Always check furniture sizes before buying to avoid cramped layouts.

How to mix patterns

Use the “big, medium, small” pattern rule and keep a shared color. This creates variety without feeling messy.

How to pick the right furniture size

Scale matters. Leave 30–36 inches for walking paths and avoid furniture that is too small for the room.

Mistakes to avoid

Buying without measuring, rugs that are too small, and relying on one overhead light are the most common beginner mistakes.

How to create a cohesive space

Repeat 2–3 colors and 2–3 materials (wood, metal, linen). Consistency is what makes spaces feel designed.

Simple Room Makeovers

Makeovers don’t need to be expensive. Small changes can make a room feel brighter, warmer, and more finished.

Living Room Refresh

  • Add a bigger rug (front legs on at minimum)
  • Layer lighting: overhead + lamp + accent
  • Repeat an accent color in pillows and art
  • Add one large statement piece (mirror or artwork)

Budget tip: Swap pillow covers + add a throw blanket for a fast upgrade.

Bedroom Cozy Upgrade

  • Use layered bedding (neutral base + texture)
  • Add matching bedside lighting
  • Hang art at eye level
  • Use warm bulbs for a soft glow

Budget tip: Matching lamps make the room feel intentional instantly.
