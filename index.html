Design Styles

Not sure what your style is yet? Start here—each style includes a quick description to help you recognize what you love.

Modern

Clean lines, open space, and a “less but better” look. Modern rooms often use neutral colors, simple shapes, and intentional decor.

Minimalist

Airy, calm, and clutter-free. Minimalist spaces focus on function, light, and a few well-chosen pieces that feel peaceful.

Boho

Warm, layered, and full of personality. Boho mixes textures, patterns, plants, and natural materials for a cozy collected vibe.

Farmhouse

Comfortable and classic with rustic touches. Farmhouse often blends soft neutrals, wood tones, and vintage details.

Beginner Topics

Small skills make the biggest difference. These guides help you avoid common mistakes and build confidence fast.

How to choose a color scheme

Pick 1 main neutral + 1 supporting neutral + 1 accent. Repeat those colors around the room for an instantly cohesive look.

How to measure a room

Measure wall lengths, doorways, and key walkways. Always confirm furniture sizes before buying to avoid cramped layouts.

How to mix patterns

Use the “big, medium, small” rule and keep a shared color. That creates variety without the room feeling chaotic.

How to pick the right furniture size

Balance is everything—avoid pieces that are too tiny or too bulky. Leave breathing room for walking paths and door swings.

Mistakes to avoid

Too-small rugs, harsh overhead lighting, and buying furniture without measuring are the biggest beginner traps—fixable with a plan.

Simple room makeover planning

Start with what you already have, choose a clear goal, then update one layer at a time: lighting → textiles → decor.
