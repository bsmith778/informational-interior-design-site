Interior Design Styles

Explore four popular styles. Each section includes what the style feels like, key features, and beginner-friendly starter steps.

Modern

Modern interior design is a style characterized by simplicity, functionality, and an emphasis on clean lines and open spaces. This approach to design emerged in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. One of the key principles of modern interior design is minimalism, which involves using only essential elements to create a streamlined and clutter-free environment.

Vibe: Clean, calm, and intentional.

  • Simple shapes and clean lines
  • Neutral colors with one statement piece
  • Less clutter, more open space

Beginner start: Pick 1 accent color and repeat it in 3 places (pillows, art, decor).

Minimalist

Minimalist style of interior design is rooted in the principle of “less is more.” This style focuses on creating spaces that are both serene and efficient. Natural light plays a crucial role in minimalist interiors. Large windows and strategically placed mirrors amplify the light, creating a bright and airy atmosphere. Minimalist style fosters a harmonious environment by merging aesthetic simplicity with practical design, making it a timeless choice for modern living.

Vibe: Bright, airy, and clutter-free.

  • Only keep what you use or love
  • Light neutrals and simple furniture
  • Clean surfaces and hidden storage
  • Restrained color palette, typically consisting of whites, grays, and neutral tones

Beginner start: Remove 5 unnecessary items from one room to instantly “clean up” the look.

Bohemian

Bohemian “Boho” style is a captivating interior design approach that embraces eclecticism and free-spirited aesthetics. This style draws inspiration from diverse cultures and artistic expressions, creating a harmonious blend of colors, textures, and patterns. Boho style encourages the incorporation of vintage and handmade items, which contribute to its charmingly imperfect and lived-in feel. Plants are another essential element, infusing the space with life and enhancing its connection to nature.

Vibe: Cozy, relaxed, and collected.

  • Warm earth tones, natural textures (rattan, linen, jute)
  • Intricate patterns, bold colors, plants, and layered textiles
  • Self-expressional pieces that brings out your adventurous side

Beginner start: Add 2 textures (woven basket + throw blanket) for instant warmth.

Farmhouse

Farmhouse style interior design is known for its warm, inviting atmosphere and its emphasis on simplicity and functionality. This design style draws inspiration from traditional rural farmhouses, blending rustic charm with modern sensibilities to create a cozy, welcoming feeling. A key aspect of farmhouse style is its neutral color palette, typically consisting of whites, grays, and beiges. It often includes open shelving, apron sinks, and large dining tables.

Vibe: Warm, welcoming, and homey.

  • Warm wood, soft neutrals, rustic decor
  • Simple patterns like stripes/checks
  • Functional pieces that look cozy

Beginner start: Swap to warm bulbs and add a simple runner rug for cozy farmhouse charm.

Next step: Start with one style as your “base,” then add small touches from another style.
